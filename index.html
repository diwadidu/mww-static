This weekend

Jump through ice, hunt for bocks.

Winter Festival in Bayfield, Wis. The Lake Superior festival features a candlelight ski/snowshoe, polar plunge, botany hike, ice-rescue demo and whitefish-liver feed.

Bock Fest in New Ulm, Minn. There's a parade of mythical Germanic characters, a hunt for billy-goat heads on the Schell Brewery grounds and beyond and a big party afterward with beer, live music and a bonfire. March 5.

World's Longest Weenie Roast in Cable, Wis. Lakewoods Resorts hosts this benefit on Lake Namekagon, which includes snowmobile speed runs, ice bowling, a weenie-eating contest and a frozen weenie polar plunge. March 4-5.

Artigras in Green Bay, Wis. This fine arts festival in KI Convention Center includes artist demonstrations, children's activities, dance, jazz and classical music. March 5-6.

Prime time on the Gunflint Trail

On Minnesota's Gunflint Trail, this is prime time. Resist the urge to stay inside and wait for spring; head north now for the best cross-country skiing, snowshoeing and snowmobiling of the season.

What to do: Ski 200 kilometers of superbly groomed trails into April. Snowshoe into the Boundary Waters and on trails.

Events to catch: March 13, [Dog Days of Winter] (http://www.trailcenterlodge.com/dog-days-of-winter.html) sled-dog derby.

Details: For more, see Winter on Minnesota's Gunflint Trail.

This weekend

Hoist a beer, race a fish.

Grumpy Old Men Festival in Wabasha, MN There's always good eagle-viewing in this Mississippi River town, and during the festival, there will be an ice-fishing contest, polar plunge and minnow races. Feb. 26.

Bockfest at Capital Brewery in Middleton, WIThis Madison-area brewery celebrates with the Running of the Blondes 1K race, a fish toss, music, costumes and, of course, beer. Feb. 26.

Chili Ice Walk in Racine, Wis. Between noon and 4 p.m., walk downtown to see ice sculpting, sample chili from eight restaurants and go on a scavenger hunt in shop windows. Feb. 26.

Leech Lake Frostfest in Walker, MN This town on Leech Lake offers fat-bike demos, dog-sled rides, a snow-golf course, lumberjack races, children's games and craft beer. Feb. 26.

For more, see our Events Calendar.

Piles of snow in the Porkies

On Michigan's Upper Peninsula, along Lake Superior, Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park has lavish piles of lovely snow when the rest of the region has been reduced to slush.

What to do: Ski downhill on 42 runs, ski cross-country on 42 kilometers of trails or snowshoe anywhere.

Events to catch: Feb. 26, Snowburst Winter Carnival with candlelight ski/snowshoe and fireworks.

Where to stay: A yurt or the handsome Kaug Wudjoo Lodge on Union Bay.

Details: For more, see White gold in the Porkies.
